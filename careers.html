§ 01 · Why MEC

Four things we believe
about the work.

If you recognise yourself in these four statements — and you're tired of tutoring platforms that treat teaching like a gig economy — we should talk.

i.

Engineers first, teachers second

Every MEC Principal Tutor is also a working engineer. You do real design work, real calculations, real deadlines — and bring that currency into every session.

ii.

Taught how to teach

You'll be trained on the MEC Lesson Engine — published-research-backed pedagogy that makes lessons ten times more effective than "just explain it again, slower."

iii.

Documented everything

The Tutor Protocol. The question banks. The progress-report templates. Nothing is improvised, nothing is passed down by folklore. You get tools that actually work.

iv.

Capped intake · fair pay

We cap your client load at a number that protects your preparation time. You are paid for planning, reports, and WhatsApp support — not just the session hour.

§ 03 · The Process

Four stages,
serious throughout.

No aptitude tests, no group interviews. Instead: a review of your engineering credentials, a teaching demo, a technical problem-set under time, and a meeting with the founder. The whole thing takes about three weeks.

Start your application
Stage 01Week 1

Application & credentials review

You send a CV, a one-page statement of teaching philosophy, and proof of your engineering qualifications. We reply within five working days with a yes or a no, either way.

Stage 02Week 2

Teaching demo · 30 min

We give you a topic 48 hours ahead. You plan a 30-minute lesson segment using the MEC Lesson Engine template. You deliver it on Zoom. We watch for clarity, structure, and calibrated uncertainty.

Stage 03Week 2 – 3

Technical problem set · 90 min

Unseen problems in your specialism, under timed conditions. We don't mark for the final answer alone — we mark for method, reasoning, and honest annotation of uncertainty.

Stage 04Week 3

Founder interview · Offer

A conversation, not a grilling. Values, expectations, logistics. If we both say yes, you sign the Tutor Protocol and start with a paired-tutoring induction week before taking your first client.

§ 04 · Apply
