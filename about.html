§ 01
A Letter from the Founder

Most tutoring is improvised.
It has no business being so.

In practising engineering, I was trained to treat every problem like it mattered — because the building stands or falls, the bracket holds or fails, the HVAC system delivers or poisons the air. There are standards. There are drawings. There is a record. A junior engineer who improvises is a liability; a senior one who improvises is fired.

Then I looked at how most tutoring is done. A university student, fresh off their own exam, is sent into a room with a matriculant. There is no lesson plan. There is no retrieval practice protocol. There is no record of what was covered, what was mastered, what was missed. The parent pays. The student's grade either moves or it doesn't — and when it doesn't, nobody can explain why, because there is nothing to examine.

I built Makhuza Educational Consultants to solve that problem from first principles. Every MEC tutor is a qualified, practising engineer. Every session runs on a four-segment Lesson Engine built from published cognitive-science research — retrieval practice, spacing, interleaving, worked-to-faded examples. Every session leaves a paper trail: the marked warm-up, the scored exam set, the error log. The method is the product.

We are priced like engineers because we are engineers. We are priced below global consulting firms because South Africa, and the students in it, deserve the international standard without the international price tag. Our clients are not buying tutoring. They are buying an engineer's hour, applied to a problem that matters — a matric exam, a university module, a career launch. And they are paying us to do it to a standard.

— Isheanesu Makhuza, Pr.Eng
Founder & Principal Tutor

§ 03 · The Lesson Engine

Ninety minutes.
Four segments. Nothing improvised.

The MEC Lesson Engine is built from three decades of cognitive-science research on what actually makes learning stick. Each segment has a specific cognitive job. Skipping one does not save time — it breaks the chain.

The research behind it.

Every component of the Engine is drawn from published work in cognitive science — retrieval practice (Roediger & Karpicke), spacing (Cepeda et al.), interleaving (Rohrer & Taylor), and worked-to-faded examples (Sweller, Renkl). None of this is novel in the laboratory; what is novel is its systematic application inside a 90-minute commercial tutoring session.

Most tutors have never heard of these studies. Every MEC tutor can cite the relevant paper and defend their use of the technique. That is the difference between trained and untrained instruction — and you can feel it in the first five minutes of a session.

Segment A0 · 15 min

Diagnostic warm-up

Mixed, spaced retrieval questions from the last 2 – 4 weeks. Silent work for 10 minutes, fast mark, circle every sub-topic with ≥ 2 errors. This is how we start — with evidence, not a chat.

Segment B15 · 45 min

Worked → faded → independent

The tutor works one example fully, narrating reasoning. The second example is "faded" — the student fills in gaps. The third is independent. By minute 45, the student owns the method, not just the answer.

Segment C45 · 75 min

Exam-conditions performance set

15-mark mini-set, blueprint-weighted to match the real paper. Pace guide ~1 mark per minute. Mark in-session. No hiding from performance under time pressure.

Segment D75 · 90 min

Error log · micro-plan · homework

Classify every miss by type (misconception, slip, method). Write the if/then fix. Assign targeted homework in-session — nothing left to memory or email. The next session's warm-up is already on the page.
